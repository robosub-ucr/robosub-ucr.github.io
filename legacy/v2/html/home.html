UCR Robosub is an autonomous underwater vehicle project, competing internationally in RoboNation's RoboSub competition. The project started in Fall 2015, and has been active since.


Each year we create a completely autonomous submarine capable of performing a number of tasks entirely on its own: we place it in the water, it searches for and completes objectives, and returns to a surface point. The sheer volume of design and construction needed requires a large interdisciplinary team working collaboratively to share expertise and communication throughout the process.

The project exposes all students to utilizing their coursework skills, working with others outside their major, learning material not taught in lectures, and granting extensive engineering experience with a large-scale project with real-world applications. For the 2017-2018 year, UCR Robosub had ~20 members on the team, across almost every engineering major, and several non-engineers.
